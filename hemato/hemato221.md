# Question
Hemolytic anemia presents with **low** haptoglobin, **high** bilirubin, and **high** LDH; also, with hemoglobinuria

---

# Answer
Haptoglobin binds to hemoglobin, so its absence means that hemoglobin was destroyed. LDH is elevated because it's released when red blood cells are destroyed. Heme is broken down to bilirubin
