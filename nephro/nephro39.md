# Question
About 25% of adults with PSGN develop **rapidly progressive glomerulonephritis**

---

# Answer
mostly, children are affected by PSGN; However, adult-onset PSGN = poor prognosis Triad: Gross hematuria (tea- or cola-colored urine) Edema (periorbital, generalized) Hypertension  Urinalysis: + protein, + blood, ± RBC casts Serum: ↓ C3 & possible ↓ C4 ↑ Serum creatinine ↑ Anti-DNase B & ↑ AHase ↑ ASO & ↑ anti-NAD (from preceding pharyngitis) AHase = antihyaluronidase;  anti-DNase B = antideoxyribonuclease B;  anti‑NAD = antinicotinamide-adenine dinucleotidase;  ASO = antistreptolysin O.
