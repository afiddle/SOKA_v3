# Question
In a baby whose mother had untreated gestational diabetes, upon delivery neonates can develop transient **hypoglycemia** ~fetal hyperglycemia & hyperinsulinemia

---

# Answer
"""not the mother's fault that you're always hungry"" - Î² cell hyperplasia in the fetus leads to fetal hyperinsulinism, and thus post-partum hypoglycemia can occur once placental transfer of maternal glucose occurs   - this typically resolves within 3-7 days of birth as hyperinsulinemia remits"
