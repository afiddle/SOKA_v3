# Question
What is the likely diagnosis in a patient that develops pallor, tachycardia, and severe hypertension without fever after induction of anesthesia? The patient has a history of frequent headaches and hypertension.   **Pheochromocytoma**

---

# Answer
- episodes of pheochromocytoma can be precipitated by medications, especially anesthetic agents, and surgical procedures; normal temperature helps differentiate pheochromocytoma from thyroid storm (typically elevated temp.) - administering Î²-blockers worsens the HTN
